FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Is Telegram Drive really unlimited?

Yes. Files live on Telegram's servers, which currently impose no per-user storage cap. You're bound only by Telegram's per-file size limit (4 GB for free accounts, 4 GB / large messages for Premium) and Telegram Drive's daily 250 GB transfer cap.

Where is my data stored?

Files stay on Telegram's data centers. Telegram Drive only stores a local Telethon session (so you don't have to log in every time), bandwidth tracking, and an optional preview cache. None of your files are uploaded anywhere outside Telegram.

Do I need a separate Telegram account?

No — Telegram Drive uses your existing Telegram account. Your "Saved Messages" become the root folder; private channels you create are folders.

What's the REST API for?

It exposes /api/v1/files, /api/v1/files/{id}, and /api/v1/files/{id}/download behind an X-API-Key. Useful for plugging your Telegram cloud into AI agents, scripts, or automation tools.

Is Telegram Drive open source?

Yes — MIT-licensed. The full source is at github.com/caamer20/Telegram-Drive.
